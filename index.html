What's on Maggies.Today?

Why It Exists Because Randomness Is Fun

Maggies.Today exists for the sake of existence. Its intended to be a fun experiment in procuring all the randomness that is the internet within a single domain, but one where for a day, every Maggies.Today visitor will be guaranteed to see exactly the same new site together.

Which is really all just a fancy way to say: we want to see what happens when the internet has the chance at submit to our git repo.

How It Works Submit A Pull Request, And Then The Form

To be included in the Maggies lottery, first you'll need to fork the Maggies.Today repo and submit a pull request. Make note of the commit hash.

Once your pull request is submitted, visit this page to fill out your entry form. You can enter once per day, but only need to make one pull request per site you wish to enter. Simply reuse the same commit hash on subsequent days to re-enter it into the lottery.

How Are The Winners Chosen? Limited Moderation And A Lottery

Maggies.Today reserves the right to disqualify entries if they're deemed to have violated certain ethical, legal, or security restrictions, but we welcome a broad submission pool. Part of the fun of Maggies.Today is seeing all sorts of random sites from random worldviews.

Once we've applied what we hope to be limited moderation to exclude any infringing entries, we take all remaining entries and spin them through a randomizer to select the winner.

Once selected, a winner will be contacted to process the payment for their bid, and upon payment they will have their pull request approved and their site published to Maggies.Today. If a winner is unable to respond within [insert length of time here], they'll lose their spot and we will re-randomize a new winner until we've successfully completed the lottery for the day.

Or TL;DR: make sure to keep an eye on your email on days you enter so you don't lose your spot if you win.
